About Me

I've spent most of my career in the aviation industry, mainly focusing in aviation maintenance. I hold my Airframe and Powerplant License (A&P) issued by the Federal Aviation Administration. I graduated from Aviation Institute of Maintenace located at the Northeast Philadelphia Airport in 2007 and quickly got a job at Hortman Aviation as a junior Mechanic. I was one of two women in maintenance. Most of my experience lies in general aviation, working on 2 to 8 place aircraft, for flight schools and repair stations.

I wanted to switch industries to have a more challenging upward career that is based on the quality of my work and less on office politics. I would also like to have a more flexible schedule now that my kid is in school. I started to teach myself HTML and CSS through freecodecamp.org and a few courses through Coursera. I applied to the UPenn/Trilogy Bootcamp because I do better in a structured environment. I really want this to work out and so I am putting a lot of energy into switching to this industry. I am excited to once again take my fate back into my hands.

I am a bit worried about Javascript and the backend structure as it will be completely new to me, but I have faith in the Bootcamp and my classmates. I believe we will all get through this together, helping each other through graduation. My husband works in the IT industry and he claims that it is a pretty small world. So I know we will all meet again after graduation!
